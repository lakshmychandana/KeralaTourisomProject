Kerala's Stunning Wonders

Thiruvananthapuram

In addition to its cultural richness, Thiruvananthapuram boasts an abundance of natural beauty. From the serene backwaters of Poovar and Vellayani to the lush greenery of Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary and Peppara Wildlife Sanctuary, the city offers a haven for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Kollam

Kollam has been the centre of cashew industry in Kerala from the time of the Portuguese in 16th c. Having hundreds of working cashew factories in the district, Kollam still continues to be the largest processed cashew exporter in India.

Pathanamthitta

Pathanamthitta district has places known for its historical importance. Among them are, ancient Valiyakoickal Temple and Palace at Pandalam, monument of Velu Thampi Dalawa at Mannadi and the Muloor Smarakom (Muloor memorial). Pandalam was the capital of the ancient Travancore kingdom.

Alappuzha

Alappuzha, also known as Alleppey, is famous for its backwaters, boat races, and the coir industry. It's often called the "Venice of the East" due to its extensive network of canals and waterways. The region is also known for its beaches, marine products, and lush paddy fields, with some farming even taking place below sea level.

Kottayam

Kottayam district, located in central Kerala, is known as "Akshara Nagari" or "city of letters" due to its significant contributions to print media and literature. It's also renowned for its scenic beauty, including backwaters, paddy fields, and rubber plantations. Kottayam is the first town in India to achieve total literacy.

Ernakulam

Ernakulam district yields the highest revenue and the largest number of industries in the state. Ernakulam is the second most populous district in Kerala, after Malappuram (out of 14 districts). The district also hosts the highest number of international and domestic tourists in Kerala state.

Thrissur

Thrissur, often called the cultural capital of Kerala, is renowned for its rich heritage, vibrant festivals, and historical significance. It's particularly famous for its elaborate temple festivals, known as Poorams, and its role in fostering cultural relations with the rest of the world.

Palakkad

Palakkad district in Kerala is renowned for its lush landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and wildlife sanctuaries. It's often called the "Granary of Kerala" due to its agricultural prowess, and its famous Palakkad Gap, a mountain pass in the Western Ghats, influences the region's climate.

Malappuram

Malappuram is renowned for its stunning natural landscapes, where verdant hills meet silvery waterfalls and tranquil backwaters. Much sought after by nature lovers and photographers alike for its abundant natural beauty.

Kozhikode

Today Kozhikode is one of the best cities in Kerala. Endowed with lush green countrysides, serene beaches, historic sites, wildlife sanctuaries, rivers, and hills, this city enchants both its inhabitants and its guests. With its unique culture and friendly ambiance, Kozhikode is a wondrous destination for all.

Wayanad

Known for its sprawling spice plantations, mist-covered mountains, and diverse wildlife, Wayanad provides an ideal retreat for nature lovers, history enthusiasts, and thrill-seekers alike

Kannur

Kannur, also known as the "City of Looms and Lores," is special for its rich cultural heritage, particularly its handloom industry and the ritualistic art form called Theyyam. It's also known for its historical sites, beautiful beaches like Muzhappilangad Drive-in Beach, and its role in the freedom struggle. Kannur is also home to St. Angelo Fort and the Indian Naval Academy at Ezhimala

Kasarkode

Kasargod is famous for its ancient forts. Francis Buchanan, the family doctor of Arthur Wellesley, visited Kasaragod in 1800. In his travelogue, he recorded information on places like Athiraparambu, Kavvai, Nileshwaram, Bekal, Chandragiri and Manjeshwar.
